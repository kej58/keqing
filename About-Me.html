Why UX/UI?


My passion for UX/UI design stems from a deep interest in how technology intersects with user experience. As an Information Science major with a minor in Economics at the University of Pittsburgh, I've learned the importance of designing digital interfaces that are not only functional but also user-friendly and economically viable. This field uniquely blends creativity, technology, and user-centered design, offering me the opportunity to make a tangible impact in the digital world. It aligns with my drive to create intuitive, accessible, and impactful digital experiences, leveraging my skills and knowledge to innovate and improve how people interact with technology.


What is my value


As a dedicated undergraduate student in Information Science with a minor in Economics at the University of Pittsburgh, and an aspiring UX/UI designer, my core values center around creating user-centric designs that enhance user experience and accessibility. I place a strong emphasis on innovation and creativity, constantly seeking new approaches to improve digital interactions. My analytical thinking, rooted in my academic background, guides me to make data-informed design decisions. I'm committed to lifelong learning, understanding that staying current with evolving technologies and trends is crucial in my field. Collaboration and effective communication are key in my approach, as I value working with diverse teams to achieve cohesive and impactful designs. Additionally, I uphold ethical and social responsibility in my work, aiming to create inclusive and economically viable digital solutions that cater to a diverse range of users.
